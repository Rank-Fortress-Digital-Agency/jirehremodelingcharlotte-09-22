Drywall is a popular material for residential construction and repair. It’s simple to put up and can be repaired with little difficulty. Types Of Holes And How To Fix Them


Four types of drywall damage typically require repair: holes, cracks, dents, and gouges. Holes are the most common type of drywall damage and can be caused by everything from doorknobs to careless children. No matter how the hole was generated, the repair process is the same.

On the other hand, cracks are usually caused by settling foundations or poor drywall installation. These types of damages are difficult to repair and will often require the help of a professional. Dents and gouges are usually caused by moving furniture or hard impacts. These types of damage can be repaired with relative ease using a putty knife and some joint compound. The repair process is relatively straightforward, no matter what drywall damage you’re dealing with. In most cases, you’ll need a putty knife and some joint compound.

Start by cleaning the area surrounding the hole or crack. Then, using a small brush, apply a thin layer of joint compound to the site and let it dry. Once the collective mix is dry, sand it until the area is smooth. Finally, apply a second layer of joint compound and allow it to dry completely. Once dry, you can paint over the repaired area, and no one will ever know there was any damage in the first place.

Tips For Preventing Future Damage


Now that you know how to fix drywall, it’s time to learn how to prevent future damage. There are a few things you can do to prolong the life of your drywall and avoid having to repair it in the future:

Door stoppers or floor mats should be used at all home entrances to avoid scuffing the walls. Install corner guards in areas where furniture is likely to come into contact with the drywall: hang pictures and other wall decorations using drywall anchors rather than nails or screws. When moving furnishings, be careful not to strike the drywall.

If you take these precautions, you can avoid drywall damage and the need for repairs. By following this advice, you can keep your walls looking great for years.
